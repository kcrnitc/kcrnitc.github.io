Projects

Over the past year, I have been actively involved in a range of interdisciplinary projects combining robotics, artificial intelligence, and real-time systems. These projects have allowed me to apply my technical skills in practical settings, working collaboratively with diverse teams to solve complex problems.My work spans both hardware and software domains, with a focus on building practical systems that address real-world challenges. I’ve particularly explored areas like autonomous navigation, human-robot interaction, and computer vision. Some key highlights include:

Riggu-Semihumanoid Robot

Riggu is a semi-humanoid robot developed with a differential drive base and an 8-inch display for visual interaction. It uses ROS2 Nav2 for autonomous navigation and features a custom GUI with face detection, user sign-in options, and chatbot integration. Riggu is designed for smooth indoor mobility and engaging human-robot interaction, making it suitable for smart assistance and demonstration purposes.

Robocon 24

ABU Robocon 2024, the competition revolved around the theme “Harvest Day”, inspired by traditional Vietnamese rice cultivation on terraced fields. Participating teams were tasked with designing two complementary robots to simulate the stages of rice farming—from planting and harvesting seedlings to transporting harvested grains for storage. A dual-robot system was developed to automate rice harvesting. Both robots were designed to work in tandem to simulate real-world agricultural processes.

Drainage Cleaning Robot

Designed and developed a semi-autonomous robot capable of cleaning drainage systems by collecting floating waste and breaking down solid particles for smoother flow. The robot is equipped with a waste collection mechanism that lifts and stores debris from the surface of the drainage water. Additionally, a rotating shredder is mounted at the front to fragment solid waste materials, preventing blockage and reducing manual intervention.
